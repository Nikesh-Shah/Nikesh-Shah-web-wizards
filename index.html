CYBER THREATS IN NEPAL

Know what's
targeting you.

These are the most common threats facing Nepali internet users today

01

Phishing Attacks

Fake emails, SMS, and lookalike websites impersonating banks, NTC, Ncell, and government portals to steal your login credentials.

HIGH RISK
02

Social Media Scams

Fake Facebook lotteries, WhatsApp job offers, Instagram "investment" schemes — targeting Nepali users every single day.

HIGH RISK
03

Mobile Banking Fraud

Vishing calls posing as eSewa, Khalti, or NMB Bank officials requesting OTPs, PINs, and account details under false pretenses.

HIGH RISK
04

Identity Theft

Misuse of your citizenship card number, passport data, and personal details for fraudulent loan applications and SIM registrations.

MEDIUM RISK
05

Cyber Harassment

Online bullying, non-consensual intimate image sharing, and digital stalking — rising sharply among youth and women in Nepal.

MEDIUM RISK
06

Malware & Ransomware

Malicious apps from unofficial sources silently locking your device or stealing contacts, photos, and banking credentials.

    YOUR DIGITAL RIGHTS

    Rights you
    deserve online.

    Every Nepali citizen has fundamental digital rights protected by law

    01

    Right to Privacy

    Your personal data, messages, and online activities cannot be accessed without lawful authority. Explicitly protected under Nepal's Privacy Act 2075 BS.

    Privacy Act 2075
    02

    Right to Digital Expression

    Freedom of speech extends fully to the digital realm. You may express opinions online within the bounds of law, free from unjust censorship and surveillance.

    Constitution 2072
    03

    Right to Information

    You have the right to access information held by public bodies and internet service providers regarding your personal data and how it is used.

    RTI Act 2064
    04

    Protection from Cybercrime

    Nepal's Electronic Transaction Act 2063 and Cybercrime provisions actively protect you from online fraud, unauthorized computer access, and digital harassment.

    ETA 2063
    05

    Right to Digital Inclusion

    Equitable access to the internet and digital services is a recognized right, aligned with Nepal's Digital Nepal Framework and SDG commitments.

    SAFE DIGITAL PRACTICES

    Simple steps.
    Massive
    protection.

    These actions protect you from over 90% of common cyber attacks. Start today.

    Password Strength Checker

    Enter a password to test

    Use Strong, Unique Passwords

    Minimum 12 characters. Use a password manager. Never reuse passwords across different sites or apps.

    Enable Two-Factor Authentication

    Activate 2FA for your email, eSewa, Khalti, banking apps, and all social media accounts immediately.

    Verify Before You Click

    Never click suspicious links in SMS, WhatsApp, or emails. Verify the sender's identity carefully before acting.

    Keep All Software Updated

    Regularly update your phone OS, all apps, and antivirus software to patch known security vulnerabilities.

    Secure Your WiFi Network

    Avoid public WiFi for banking. Use WPA3 encryption at home. Always change default router passwords.

    Control Your Personal Data

    Limit oversharing on social media. Review app permissions regularly. Maximize privacy settings everywhere.

    Report Cyber Incidents

    Contact Nepal Police Cyber Bureau at 1930 or file online at cybercrime.police.gov.np.
